TL;DR — This week's insight

Three things first-pass teams do before entering the lab — that make this conversation unnecessary.

1.  EMC is on the project plan before component selection — not bolted on six weeks before the lab

2.  Pre-compliance testing happens at prototype stage, when changes cost €2–5K instead of €40–80K

3.  The risk conversation happens with leadership before the test — so the failure, if it comes, is never a surprise

The technical failure is rarely the worst part.

The worst part is sitting across from your CEO — or your board — and explaining why the product that was supposed to ship this quarter isn't shipping. Why the roadmap is now three months behind. Why there's a €60K line item nobody planned for.

EMC test failures aren't just lab problems. They land on the desk of whoever is accountable for getting the product to market.

The question that's hard to answer

Most engineering directors can handle the technical side. They know what failed, what the fix is, and how long the re-spin will take.

What's harder to answer is the other question: "How did we not see this coming?"

Because the honest answer — that EMC was treated as a final checkpoint rather than a design constraint — is not one anyone wants to give in that room.

The real cost

€40–80K per failed EMC test cycle, plus 3–6 months of schedule impact. The engineering problem is solvable. The business conversation that follows it is harder to recover from.

The teams that never have this conversation

They didn't have better engineers or a bigger budget. They had EMC in the plan from the start — before component selection, before layout, before anyone was locked into a board revision they couldn't change.

When they walked into the lab, they already knew the likely failure modes. They'd run pre-compliance at prototype stage. The CEO conversation, if it ever came, was one they'd already had — before the test, not after it.

Most teams

EMC added to schedule 4–6 weeks before submission

Pre-compliance skipped to save time

Failure is a surprise — to the team and to leadership

Re-spin budget approved after the fact

First-pass teams

EMC in the plan before component selection

Pre-compliance at prototype stage (€2–5K, not €40–80K)

Leadership briefed on risk before the lab — no surprises

Re-spin budget already in the project plan

The difference between those two teams isn't what happens in the lab. It's what happened six months before they booked it.
